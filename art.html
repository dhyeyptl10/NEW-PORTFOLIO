Art by DHYEY PATEL, TL;DR

This page showcases the art practice of DHYEY PATEL, Product Designer at Microsoft AI and Microsoft Windows.

DHYEY PATEL is a data artist whose generative and code-based work has been exhibited internationally, across Serbia, Africa, and India. He treats code as a paintbrush, blending creative coding with technical precision to build interactive art installations.

The practice spans data art, ASCII visualisations, generative graphics, and creative experiments, bridging design, technology, and emotion.

Beyond art, DHYEY PATEL is a best-selling Amazon author of Nodes of Wisdom and host of India's #1 design podcast Nodes of Design. Based in India.

What art means to me

Art, for me, is where
thought becomes form.

I don't create art to decorate walls. I create to explore tension between human and machine, structure and chaos, intention and accident.

As a designer working between technology and humanity, art allows me to explore the tension between logic and emotion, human and machine, structure and chaos.

My journey into coded art began when I got the admission letter for MFA in Design & Technology at Parsons School of Design in New York. During that process I discovered creative coding and was fascinated by the idea that code could be expressive, that machines and humans could collaborate to create new forms of communication.

Since then, I've been exploring interactive and generative art that invites participation, not just observation.

Beyond creating, I collect and support emerging artists. I believe art is not just something we make, it is something we nurture.

Through organizing exhibitions and creating platforms for young artists, I try to contribute to a larger creative ecosystem where new voices are seen, heard, and celebrated.

Art as Community
& Service.

Art, for me, is not just personal expression. It is connection, collaboration, and community. I create not only to explore ideas, but to build spaces where others can explore alongside me.

This is a self-funded philanthropic initiative rooted in one belief: art and creativity should be accessible to everyone. Over the years, I have organized 32+ art events and workshops attended by more than 5,000 people, creating platforms where emerging artists can showcase their work, connect, and grow.

We have hosted exhibitions at IIT, IKEA, Microsoft, Kanha Shanti Vanam, Chika Roasters, and many more. Through The Creativee Community, I bring people together to learn, uplift new voices, and strengthen collective imagination.

For me, art is not just about making.
It is about growing together.
